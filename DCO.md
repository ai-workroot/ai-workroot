# Developer Certificate Of Origin

AI Workroot uses a Developer Certificate of Origin style contribution rule.

By contributing to this project, you certify that you have the right to submit the contribution under the project license.

## Certification

By making a contribution, you certify that:

1. The contribution was created by you and you have the right to submit it under the Apache License, Version 2.0; or
2. The contribution is based on previous work that is compatible with the Apache License, Version 2.0 and you have the right to submit it; or
3. The contribution was provided to you by someone who certified one of the above.

You also certify that you understand the contribution and project records may be public.

## Sign-Off

Contributors should add a sign-off line to commits when practical:

```text
Signed-off-by: Your Name <your.email@example.com>
```

This follows the standard Developer Certificate of Origin convention.

## Why This Project Uses DCO

AI Workroot is intended to remain open and easy to contribute to.

A DCO-style rule keeps contribution rights clear without requiring a heavy contributor license agreement at the beginning of the project.
