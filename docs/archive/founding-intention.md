# Founding Intention

> This is an early founding note.
> The canonical philosophy document is now [Project Philosophy](../project-philosophy.md).

AI Workroot is not only about productivity.

It starts from a deeper question:

> In a time when AI is developing rapidly, how can human beings embrace AI without losing themselves?

Many AI products help people answer a question or finish a task. That is useful, but it is not enough.

Human life is continuous. A person works, learns, struggles, decides, fails, recovers, loves, creates, and grows over time. If AI only lives inside disconnected chats, then much of that growth disappears after each session.

AI Workroot tries to create a different foundation.

It asks AI to become a long-term collaborator inside a durable Workroot, while keeping the person as the subject. The Workroot should remember what happened, preserve why decisions were made, turn useful results into knowledge, and let future AI agents continue from the last meaningful state.

## Philosophical Foundation

The philosophical foundation is simple:

- people should remain the center
- AI should help people become clearer, steadier, and more capable
- memory, knowledge, decisions, values, and growth should belong to the person
- ordinary people should have an easy starting point in the AI era
- human work should become accumulated understanding, not scattered fragments
- after lessons are learned, people should be allowed to release old pain and keep moving forward

AI Workroot does not claim to be the only answer for everyone. It aims to become a correct starting point for many people: simple enough to begin, rigorous enough to grow, open enough to adapt, and human enough to keep love, peace, and long-term continuity at the center.

## Philosophy-Led, Engineering-Grounded

AI Workroot is philosophy-led and engineering-grounded.

It does not start by asking which database, agent framework, vector index, or user interface is most advanced.

It starts by asking:

- What should remain human in the age of AI?
- How can a person preserve continuity without being locked into one tool?
- How can work become memory, knowledge, judgment, and growth?
- How can AI help without replacing the subject?

Engineering is essential, but it is not the highest layer. Engineering exists to make the philosophy usable, reliable, portable, and testable.

The design order is:

1. philosophy: human continuity, agency, memory, responsibility, growth, and love
2. protocol: identity, daily loop, task lifecycle, Mind model, release policy, and context policy
3. structure: files, directories, templates, registries, and configuration
4. tools: agents, models, databases, indexes, skills, MCP servers, and plugins
5. product: future applications, services, interfaces, and commercial offerings

If a technical choice conflicts with the human-centered purpose, the purpose wins.

If a tool makes the project look more advanced but makes ordinary people less able to begin, the tool should wait.

## Core Idea

```text
human intention -> AI-assisted work -> result -> memory -> knowledge -> growth
```

AI Workroot treats the person as the subject.

AI is a collaborator. Models are replaceable. Agents are replaceable. Tools are replaceable. The Workroot is the continuity layer that remains.

Over time, a Workroot should become a living external mind:

- it knows what you are working on
- it remembers what happened
- it preserves why decisions were made
- it separates temporary records from durable knowledge
- it keeps wrong or outdated beliefs visible instead of silently burying them
- it lets people release painful past context after the lesson has been preserved
- it helps the next AI agent continue from the last meaningful state

## Ordinary People Should Have A Real Starting Point

AI should not only belong to programmers, researchers, or people who already understand agent frameworks.

A person should be able to download a Workroot, tell an AI who they are and what they want to do, and begin working immediately.

The internal system can be rigorous, but the first user experience must be simple.

## Subject And Direction Come First

Before formal work begins, every Workroot must define its subject.

The subject can be a person, team, role, project, or organization. The first guidance can be small:

```text
This Workroot represents ...
The AI should act as ...
It should help with ...
It should respect ...
```

This is not a rigid persona. It is the first anchor that lets AI understand who it is serving, what direction matters, and how future memory and knowledge should accumulate.

## Do The Right Things, Then Do Things Right

Choosing what is worth doing depends on values, life context, creativity, courage, and judgment.

AI Workroot cannot replace that responsibility. It can, however, help people clarify direction, execute tasks, preserve decisions, learn from outcomes, and keep improving their way of working.

This is why AI Workroot separates meaning from execution.

- identity anchors who the work serves
- principles guide what should matter
- tasks carry present action
- decisions preserve choice and responsibility
- reflections turn experience into understanding
- knowledge preserves reusable learning
- release lets unnecessary pain leave active recall

The loop is not only about doing more. It is about doing, understanding, preserving, and continuing with more clarity.

## Remember, Learn, Then Release

People make mistakes, pass through difficult periods, and carry painful memories.

AI Workroot should not force every painful detail to remain active forever. When an experience has already produced useful learning, the lesson can remain as knowledge, decision, principle, or pattern, while the unnecessary pain can be released from normal recall.

Release is not denial, irresponsibility, or pretending the past never happened. It is a conscious act: keep the growth, stop carrying the raw pain as active identity, and return attention to the present and the future.

This is a user choice. Some people may want to preserve painful memories as part of their history. Others may want to forget, let go, and move forward. Some may want to keep only a small tombstone: a quiet memorial marker that can be visited intentionally, without keeping the raw pain active. AI Workroot should support all of these choices.

The default principle is:

> Preserve the lesson. Release the unnecessary pain. Live in the present. Move toward the future.

## Simple For Users, Strict For Agents

Users should not need to understand every directory, index, and protocol on day one.

They should be able to say:

```text
Help me work inside this AI Workroot.
```

Behind the scenes, AI agents should follow strict rules:

- classify the request
- create or update tasks when needed
- preserve handoff context
- promote useful results into memory or knowledge
- maintain indexes
- respect privacy and portability
- never trap important context inside one agent's private memory

## Files First, Databases Later

Markdown, CSV, JSON, and local files are the source of truth in the current public seed.

Databases, vector search, graph indexes, MCP servers, and specialized skills are optional accelerators. They should improve retrieval and scale, but they must not become the only place where a person's knowledge lives.

## Small Context, Long Memory

A Workroot should be able to grow for years without forcing every new session to read the whole past.

Startup context must stay small. Old task logs, raw data, archives, released context, tombstones, and generated databases should not enter normal context by default.

The Workroot should use summaries, handoffs, indexes, lifecycle status, and optional local accelerators so long memory remains useful without becoming noise.

## Personal Workroots Come First

AI Workroot is personal-first.

Its first responsibility is to serve:

- a person building a lifelong AI Workroot
- a person preserving knowledge across tools
- a person working through long-term goals, decisions, and unfinished projects
- a person who wants AI help without giving ownership of continuity to one product

The underlying protocol may later support team, role, project, or organizational forms. Those directions should be treated as future expansions, not the center of the current product promise.
