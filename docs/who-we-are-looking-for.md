# Who We Are Looking For

AI Workroot is philosophy-led and engineering-grounded.

We are looking for people who care about how individuals can live and work with AI over time without losing continuity, ownership, memory, knowledge, or agency.

This is not a broad recruiting list. AI Workroot needs a small number of exceptional individual contributors who can help shape the project at the level of philosophy, AI engineering, and consumer product experience.

## Core Contributor Profile

We are especially looking for people who are already expert-level in at least one of these areas and deeply respectful of the others:

- philosophy of human life with AI: deep thinking about continuity, agency, memory, knowledge, responsibility, forgetting, privacy, love, growth, and the meaning of AI-assisted work
- AI engineering architecture: expert ability to design simple, rigorous, maintainable, portable systems across agents, models, tools, local runtimes, context, memory, indexes, and extension boundaries
- consumer AI product experience: expert judgment about how ordinary people start, understand, trust, and keep using an AI product without needing to learn its internal architecture

The strongest contributors may combine all three:

```text
philosophical depth + AI engineering excellence + consumer AI product judgment
```

That combination is rare. It is also the kind of thinking this project needs.

## What We Value

Good long-term contributors should be able to:

- protect the human-centered spirit of the project
- think from first principles instead of chasing tool trends
- simplify ordinary user experience without weakening the kernel
- design for people who are not programmers
- make rigorous engineering decisions that remain portable across agents, models, and operating systems
- preserve privacy, ownership, continuity, and emotional boundaries
- disagree clearly and improve the project through reasoned judgment
- care about helping ordinary people use AI to work, remember, grow, and live better

## Contribution Areas

The first areas where exceptional contributors can help are:

- personal Workroot system architecture
- kernel contracts and compatibility boundaries
- context economy, memory, knowledge, retrieval, and indexing
- agent behavior across Codex, Claude Code, Cursor, and future AI agents
- ordinary-user onboarding, first-use flow, continuation, and trust
- privacy, export, import, portability, and long-term ownership
- public writing that explains the project without diluting its philosophy

## What We Do Not Want

AI Workroot should not become:

- a tool-centered framework
- a provider-specific product
- a mandatory hosted service
- a database-first system
- a collection of disconnected templates
- a project that only programmers can use
